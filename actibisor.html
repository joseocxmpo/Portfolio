Actibisor

Actibisor is a bilingual web platform dedicated to promoting Spanish literacy among the deaf. It offers a variety of interactive games and tools while giving top priority to accessibility and customization to cater to the unique needs of its users. The project is unwaveringly committed to closing educational disparities and fostering social inclusion.

Research

Problem

Deaf individuals frequently encounter substantial barriers when it comes to accessing education. These barriers include the lack of accessible and effective tools for developing their literacy skills. As a result, they may experience feelings of exclusion, missed educational opportunities, and a subpar learning experience.

Findings

Based on insights gathered from co-design sessions with the deaf community, along with accessibility and usability surveys, several key findings have surfaced:

  1. Absence of a Bilingual Interface: Existing platforms provide information solely in Spanish, which is counterproductive for a platform designed to facilitate Spanish learning.
  2. Educational Resources Gap: There is a scarcity of educational materials tailored to accommodate deaf individuals in school settings, resulting in a suboptimal learning experience.
  3. Differing Perception of Information: Deaf individuals often interpret information differently from those with hearing abilities. Elements such as emojis, buttons, and even words can be subject to varied interpretations, potentially leading to miscommunication or misunderstanding.

Goal

The goal of Actibisor is to elevate the educational journey of deaf individuals by creating an inclusive platform that fosters effective communication, supports social inclusion, and provides seamless access to essential information, educational resources, and opportunities. Actibisor aims to simplify the learning process, promoting a sense of belonging and empowering deaf learners to maximize their educational experience.

Market research

"Current platforms for educating Spanish literacy among the deaf community"

ALAS and InterSign

Strengths:

  • Specific educational focus in their respective areas, whether it's literacy or sign language.
  • Flexibility in terms of time and learning pace, making it adaptable to user needs.
  • Use of interactive technology for practice and exercises, which can enhance communication skills.

Weaknesses:

  • Potential limitation in educational content, restricting learning opportunities beyond their specific focuses.
  • Technological dependence, which may not be suitable for all contexts or situations.
  • Technical barriers, as not all individuals may have access to the necessary technology to use these platforms, leading to potential exclusions in certain cases.
